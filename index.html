Case Study • Making Language Tech Truly Accessible

Evaluating how conversational AI supports “Easy‑to‑Read” Spanish texts for users with cognitive disabilities

Overview

Context. As part of my PhD in Computational Linguistics, I explored how large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT can support adaptation of standard Spanish texts into the Lectura Fácil (Easy‑to‑Read) variant — a simplified format designed for people with cognitive disabilities, low literacy, or language barriers. Manually producing these texts is time‑intensive and costly; this study examined whether AI could help while preserving accessibility.

  • My role: Lead researcher — methodology design, prompt experiments, quantitative & qualitative evaluations, synthesis of design implications.
  • Timeline: 6–8 months
  • Collaboration: Hochschule Darmstadt • HiTZ Center (Basque Country)

Research Question

How well can ChatGPT adapt Spanish news texts into Easy‑to‑Read, and what are the usability and accessibility implications?

Methods

Approach. I selected 10 Spanish news articles and ran multiple adaptation experiments with different prompt styles. Evaluation combined automated readability metrics, a manual checklist review, and user testing with participants with cognitive disabilities.

  • Prompt experiments guided by UNE 153101:2018 EX Easy‑to‑Read guidelines.
  • Automated readability — lexical & syntactic complexity (e.g., rare word rate, sentence length).
  • Manual checklist — fluency, simplicity, meaning preservation, and layout/format concerns.
  • User testing — comprehension & preference comparisons of human‑ vs AI‑adapted versions.

Key Findings & Insights

  • LLM outputs simplified vocabulary but often increased sentence length and structural complexity, which can hinder comprehension.
  • Participants consistently preferred human‑adapted texts for clarity and readability.
  • Prompt wording affected outcomes; reproducibility and consistency remain challenges for LLM‑assisted workflows.
  • Automated metrics missed layout/format aspects vital to accessibility (line spacing, sentence breaks, explicit examples).

Design Recommendations & Impact

  • Adopt a human‑in‑the‑loop workflow — AI for first pass, human review for accessibility compliance.
  • Create a readability + layout dashboard to monitor Easy‑to‑Read criteria.
  • Develop prompt templates and checklists to guide consistent adaptation.
  • Plan inclusive user testing early to validate comprehension, not just readability scores.

Reflections

  • Empathetic facilitation is essential when collaborating with participants with cognitive disabilities.
  • Mixed‑methods triangulation yields more actionable insights than any single metric.
  • Next step: prototype an interactive adaptation interface (text + speech) to evaluate real‑world tasks.
